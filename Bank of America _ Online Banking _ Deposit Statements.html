Help for Available Balance. What does this include?
Help for Available Balance

Your Available Balance is the amount of money in your checking or savings account that is currently available for you. It includes all Cleared and Processing transactions. Keep in mind that any transactions you have made but Bank of America has not yet received need to be subtracted from your Available Balance for you to know the exact amount of money you have to spend or withdraw.

The Available Balance does not include:

  • Any transaction that has not been received by the bank (check, recurring Debit Card transactions, ACH)
  • Amount of a deposit not currently available, including holds

Please reference “Tips to help avoid fees”, located on the Information & Services tab for more information about managing your fees.

Credit pending:
Help for Credit Pending. 
Help for Credit Pending

Credit Pending is the amount of a deposit which has not been made immediately available. Credit Pending items are not included in your Available Balance. They are shown in the Description column along with an estimated date when the funds may be made available if a Deposit Hold is not placed.

Deposit Holds are delays to the availability of deposited checks. If you receive one, you will receive a notice with the reason and the new date the funds will be available. The date on the Hold notice will replace the Credit Pending date for that portion of the deposit. Please refer to Bank of America’s Hold Policy for more details.

A Hold is shown as a separate transaction on your Account Details page.

Estimated balance:
Help for Estimated Balance. 
Help for Estimated Balance

Your Estimated Balance is the amount that’s estimated to be in your account once all known transactions have processed. It includes your Available Balance plus any Credit Pending and Hold amounts from check deposits (not credited to your account). This is just an estimate and not a promise you will actually have this balance on a specified date.
